Changelog

Every released version of BenchPDF, in order.

1.0.4 2026-07-23
  • The desktop app's system-tray icon is back, with its Open, About and Quit menu. It had been left out of the packaged builds since the first release, so a window with no console had no clean way to close; it now runs and quits as intended.
1.0.3 2026-07-23
  • A bordered or shaded box is no longer rebuilt as a table. Callout panels and summary boxes, like the balance panel on a bank statement, used to gain a hard border and lose their shading and alignment; they now keep their look while the text inside flows normally.
  • Bulleted lists hold their shape in more documents. Where the marker is a symbol-font glyph or a decorative letter rather than a real bullet character, each item now keeps its own line instead of running together into one paragraph.
  • Text blocks follow the spacing of the original: the blank lines between paragraphs, the line pitch, and the left and hanging indents all come through, instead of packing tight and flush to the margin.
  • Reflowed paragraphs break closer to where the source broke them, so a wrapped column drifts less and no longer runs into the heading beneath it.
  • The in-browser converter and the desktop engine now agree on more documents. A statement's summary panel, in particular, converts the same way in both.
  • The diagnostics suite grows to 354 checks across both engines, with new fixtures for bordered callouts, dingbat-marked lists and paragraph indents, and its table and list checks no longer grade the engine against its own output.
1.0.2 2026-07-20
  • Text drawn twice for weight or shadow now converts once instead of doubling, in PDF to PowerPoint and the editor alike.
  • The browser converter reconstructs word gaps in headings that never draw a space glyph, and keeps tightly tracked numbering together.
  • The diagnostics suite gains a styled-proposal fixture and fails fast on degenerate geometry, so one broken check cannot hide another.
1.0.1 2026-07-20
  • Dense tables no longer overflow the slide in PDF to PowerPoint: empty cells were quietly inflating every sparse row to PowerPoint's default line height.
  • The in-browser PDF to PowerPoint converter went live at full parity with the desktop engine, then received a fix for text inside Form XObjects, the construct many bank statements use for headers and small print.
  • The built-in diagnostics suite gained two fixtures, a dense synthetic bank statement and a form-XObject statement, and new checks for zero-height text, stacked elements and corrupted font sizes.
1.0.0 2026-07-15
  • Initial public release.
  • Local conversion hub: Word, Excel, and PowerPoint to and from PDF; images (including HEIC) to PDF; web page to PDF; PDF to images and to plain text.
  • In-browser PDF editor: click-to-edit text blocks over the original page, native table editing, export to a real, searchable PDF with untouched pages left byte-identical.
  • Office detection: Word/Excel/PowerPoint conversions are automatically disabled with a plain explanation on PCs without Microsoft Office installed, instead of failing.
  • Packaged as a self-contained Windows installer. It does not need a separate Python install or admin rights.
  • In-app About dialog with version, privacy statement, license, and the full list of bundled open-source components.

Latest release: 1.0.4, 23 July 2026. The desktop app's system-tray menu is back, and conversions keep bordered boxes as boxes and follow the source's spacing and indents.
