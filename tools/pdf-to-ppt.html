PDF to PowerPoint converter

Turn a PDF into real, editable PowerPoint slides — without sending the file anywhere.

Convert online — no install Download desktop app

Try it online — works right here for text-based PDFs; the desktop app adds native tables and chart-aware layout.

Most “PDF to PPT” tools online ask you to upload the file to their server, wait in a queue, then download the result — and somewhere in between, a document you didn't choose to share left your machine. BenchPDF does the same conversion locally: it reads the PDF, works out what's on each page, and writes a .pptx file, all inside a program running on your own PC.

The conversion is page-aware rather than one-size-fits-all. Pages that are mostly text on a plain background become real, editable PowerPoint text boxes and native tables. Pages with charts or complex artwork keep that artwork as a locked background image with the text laid on top, so you can fix a typo without redrawing a chart. Scanned pages with no selectable text are placed as images and clearly flagged, rather than pretending they're editable. BenchPDF tells you which mode it used for every page.

Because there's no upload, there's no file size cap, no queue, and no waiting on someone else's server load — the conversion runs as fast as your own PC can render the pages. It also means confidential decks, financial reports, or internal documents never pass through a third party's infrastructure at all.
