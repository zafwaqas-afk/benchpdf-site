PDF to PowerPoint Converter

This one needs the Windows app. It rebuilds paragraphs, tables and fonts, so you get a deck you can edit rather than a page of stray text boxes.

Download BenchPDF 1.0.0 for Windows Needs Windows 10 or 11. No Office required for this conversion.

What comes through

  • Paragraphs as single text boxes, so a paragraph stays one thing you can edit.
  • Tables as real PowerPoint tables, with the rows and columns intact.
  • Fonts matched to the nearest equivalent installed on your PC, not collapsed to one face.
  • Backgrounds, fills and charts, kept as the graphic layer behind the text.

Where it stops

A page with no selectable text underneath, which is what a scan is, has no words to recover. That page arrives as a picture of itself, so nothing is lost visually, but the text is not editable. Getting text out of a scan needs OCR, which BenchPDF does not do. Tables drawn with spacing alone and no ruling lines are also hard to detect, and those arrive as positioned text rather than as a table.

How to convert PDF to PowerPoint

  • Download and install BenchPDF, then open it.
  • Drop your PDF in, or pick PDF to PowerPoint from the grid.
  • The app rebuilds each page as a slide and saves a .pptx.
  • Download the result, or open the output folder from the app.

Why is this not in the browser?

Because we tried it and the output was not good enough to put our name on. Rebuilding a page as slides needs paragraph clustering, table detection, font matching and a rendered background layer. Our browser attempt produced up to 116 text boxes on a page the desktop engine renders as six, with no tables at all, so we withdrew it rather than ship it.

What happens to my tables?

A table with ruling lines is detected and rebuilt as a real PowerPoint table, so you can click a cell, retype it, and add rows. This is checked on every release by a regression suite that fails the build if any table in the test documents comes back as loose text instead.

Can I edit the text afterwards?

Yes, on any page that had selectable text. Paragraphs become PowerPoint text boxes at paragraph level rather than line level, which matters: a line-level conversion looks similar until you try to edit a sentence and find it split across five boxes that will not reflow.

What fonts does the output use?

Each source font is matched to the nearest equivalent installed on your PC, and the same source font always maps to the same target so the deck stays consistent. Where a font is substituted the app records it, and the conversion report lists every substitution it made.

Is my document uploaded anywhere?

No. The app runs on your PC, reads the PDF from your disk and writes the .pptx back to it. No server is involved in the conversion at any point.

Does it work on a Mac?

Not yet, the app is Windows only. The PDF to Images and PDF to Text tools on this site run in any browser, including on a Mac, but they do not produce slides.
