Most online "PDF to image" tools upload your file to a server, convert it there, and send the result back. This tool renders each page directly in your browser using your own device, and packages the images into a zip file you download. Nothing is transmitted anywhere. It is also the most reliable option if your PDF turns out to be a scan with no real text underneath, since it renders pixels rather than trying to read words that are not there.

How to convert PDF to images

  • Choose PNG or JPG and a resolution above, or leave the defaults.
  • Drop your PDF into the box, or click to choose one from your device.
  • Wait while each page renders as an image.
  • Click Download to save a zip file containing one image per page.

Is it safe to convert my PDF here?

Yes. Every page is rendered to an image inside your own browser, using your device's processing power. The PDF is never sent to a server, and nothing about it is transmitted anywhere. You can confirm this yourself by checking your browser's network activity while converting.

What image format do I get, PNG or JPG?

Whichever you choose before converting. PNG is the default and keeps sharp edges on text and line art with no compression loss. JPG produces a smaller file and suits photo-heavy pages. Both options are available above the drop zone, and the choice applies to every page in that PDF.

Can I choose the resolution?

Yes, in DPI: 96 for on-screen use, 150 for a good balance of quality and file size, or 300 for print-quality output. Higher DPI produces larger, sharper images but takes a little longer to render and produces a bigger download, since every pixel is generated on your own device.

Is there a file size or page limit?

No artificial limit is applied by this tool. The real constraint is your device's memory, since every page is rendered locally rather than on a server. A short PDF at 150 DPI converts in a few seconds. A long document at 300 DPI takes longer and produces a larger zip file to download.

Will the images match the PDF page exactly?

Yes. Unlike the PDF to PowerPoint tool, this one does not try to extract or rebuild anything, it renders each page pixel for pixel, the same way a PDF viewer would display it. Charts, photos, colours, and layout all come through exactly as they appear in the original PDF.

Does it work on Mac, or is it Windows only?

This online converter works on any device with a modern browser, including Mac, Windows, Linux, and Chromebooks, since it runs as JavaScript rather than a Windows program. BenchPDF also has a separate Windows desktop app with extra features, but you do not need it for this page.
