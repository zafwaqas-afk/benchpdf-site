Most online "images to PDF" tools upload your photos to a server, merge them there, and send back a PDF. This tool builds the PDF directly in your browser, using your own device. Your photos never leave it, at any point in the process.

How to convert images to PDF

  • Drop one or more JPG or PNG images into the box, or choose them from your device.
  • They are combined in the order you dropped or selected them.
  • Wait a moment while the PDF is built.
  • Click Download to save the merged PDF.

Is it safe to combine my photos here?

Yes. The PDF is assembled directly in your browser, using your own device, and your photos are never uploaded anywhere. You can check this yourself by watching your browser's network activity while the merge runs, there is no request carrying your images to a server.

What image formats can I use?

JPG and PNG. Most photos from a phone or a screenshot are already in one of these formats. HEIC photos, the default format on newer iPhones, are not supported by this online tool; BenchPDF's desktop app handles HEIC as well as JPG and PNG.

Can I control the order of the pages?

Yes. Pages come out in the same order you dropped the files, or the order they appear in your file picker if you selected several at once. To change the order, drop or select the images one at a time in the sequence you want them to appear in the final PDF.

Is there a limit on how many images I can add?

No artificial limit is applied by this tool. The practical limit is your device's memory, since everything is processed locally rather than uploaded to a server. A handful of photos merges in a second or two. Dozens of high-resolution images will take longer and use more memory.

Will the PDF keep the original image quality?

Yes. Each photo is embedded in the PDF at its original resolution and quality, with no recompression. The page size is set to match each image, so nothing is cropped, stretched, or resampled. The resulting PDF file size is roughly the same as your original photos combined.

Does it work on Mac, or is it Windows only?

This online converter works on any device with a modern browser, including Mac, Windows, Linux, and Chromebooks, since it runs as JavaScript rather than a Windows program. BenchPDF also has a separate Windows desktop app with extra features, but you do not need it for this page.
