What comes through

  • The original resolution of every image, with no recompression on the way in.
  • The order you dropped or selected the files.
  • The proportions of each photo, with the page sized to fit rather than the image cropped.
  • One page per image.

Where it stops

JPG and PNG only. HEIC photos, which is what newer iPhones produce by default, are not read here and need the desktop app. Because nothing is recompressed, a stack of high resolution photos makes a correspondingly large PDF, so if you need a small file for email you will want to shrink the images before dropping them in.

How to combine images into a PDF

  • Drop one or more JPG or PNG images into the box, or choose files.
  • They go in in the order you dropped or selected them.
  • Wait a moment while the PDF is built.
  • Click Download to save it.

Does it reduce the image quality?

No. Each photo is embedded at its original resolution with no recompression, and the page is sized to match the image rather than the image being scaled to fit a page. The resulting PDF is therefore roughly as large as the source photos added together.

How do I control the page order?

Pages come out in the order you dropped the files, or the order they appeared in the file picker. To get a specific sequence, drop them one at a time in the order you want, or rename them so they sort correctly before selecting them together.

Can I use iPhone photos?

Only if they are JPG or PNG. Newer iPhones save in HEIC by default, which this page does not read. You can either change the camera format to Most Compatible in iOS settings, export the photos as JPG first, or use the BenchPDF desktop app, which handles HEIC.

Does my file get uploaded?

No. The PDF is assembled inside your own browser from images read off your device, and never leaves it. Watching your browser's network activity during a merge will show no request carrying your photos anywhere.

How many images can I add?

No cap is set by the tool. A handful merge in about a second. Dozens of high resolution photographs take longer and use more memory, and your device's available memory is the real limit, since everything is processed locally rather than on a server.

Does it work on a Mac?

Yes. This page runs in the browser, so macOS, Windows, Linux and ChromeOS all work. The separate BenchPDF desktop app is Windows only, and is what you would need for HEIC photos.
