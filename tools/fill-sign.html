What it does

  • Opens your PDF and draws it exactly as it is, so nothing in the original changes.
  • Lets you drop text, a date, a check mark, or your signature anywhere on any page.
  • Draws a signature you make with the mouse or trackpad, or one built from your typed name.
  • Writes everything back into a new PDF that opens anywhere a PDF opens.

Where it stops

This adds your marks on top of the PDF; it does not verify who you are or attach a certified audit trail, so it is not a witnessed or notarised e-signature. When a counterparty needs proof of signer identity, use a dedicated e-signature service. For signing a document yourself and sending it back, this does the job without an account and without the file ever leaving your device.

How to fill and sign a PDF

  • Drop your PDF into the box, or choose a file.
  • Pick Text, Date, Check, or Signature, then click where you want it.
  • Drag anything to reposition it; the red handle removes it.
  • Click Download signed PDF to save the finished file.

Does my file get uploaded?

No. The PDF is opened, drawn, and rewritten inside your own browser, and the signature you draw or type never leaves the page either. Nothing about the document is transmitted, and you can confirm it by watching your browser's network activity while you work.

Does it work on a Mac?

Yes. This page runs in the browser, so macOS, Windows, Linux and ChromeOS all work. The separate BenchPDF desktop app is Windows only, but this tool does not need it.
