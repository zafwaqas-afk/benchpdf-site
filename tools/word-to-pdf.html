Word to PDF Converter

This one needs the Windows app. Your own copy of Word does the export, so the PDF looks exactly like the document on your screen.

Download BenchPDF 1.0.0 for Windows Needs Microsoft Word installed.

What comes through

  • Fonts as embedded by Word, including any installed on your PC.
  • Page breaks and pagination, matching what you see in Word.
  • Headers, footers and page numbering.
  • Embedded images, charts and shapes, drawn by Word rather than redrawn.

Where it stops

The conversion is only as current as the document you hand it. If the file has tracked changes showing or comments displayed, they render into the PDF exactly as they appear on screen, so accept or hide them first. The app also needs Word itself installed, and it drives your licensed copy rather than replacing it.

How to convert Word to PDF

  • Download and install BenchPDF.
  • Open the app and drop your .docx or .doc in, or pick Word to PDF from the grid.
  • BenchPDF opens it in your installed Word and exports the PDF.
  • Download the result, or open the output folder from the app.

Will the formatting match exactly?

Yes, because Word produces the PDF rather than a separate engine imitating Word. Fonts, column layouts, page breaks, headers and embedded objects come out as Word renders them, which avoids the drift that browser based converters introduce on anything more involved than a plain letter.

Why is this not in the browser?

A browser has no way to run Word's layout engine, so it would have to reinterpret the .docx and draw its own approximation. On a plain letter you might not notice. On multi-column layouts, unusual fonts or embedded objects you would. Driving your installed Word removes that whole class of difference.

Do I need Word installed?

Yes. Your own copy does the rendering, so Word has to be on the PC. If it is not detected, BenchPDF greys this conversion out and says why, rather than substituting a lower fidelity method without telling you.

What happens to tracked changes?

They export the way they are currently displayed. If markup is showing in Word, the PDF shows the markup. Set the document to show the final version, or accept the changes, before converting if you want a clean copy.

Is my document uploaded anywhere?

No. The app opens your document in the copy of Word already on your PC, exports it, and writes the PDF to your disk. Nothing is sent to a server during the conversion.

Does it work on a Mac?

The app is Windows only, because it automates the Windows build of Word. Word for Mac has its own Save as PDF export, which gives a comparable result for the same reason: Word is doing the rendering either way.
