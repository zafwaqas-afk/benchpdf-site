Word to PDF Converter

Uses your own installed Microsoft Word to convert, so formatting matches exactly. Free Windows desktop app, no account needed.

Download BenchPDF for Windows Free, no account. Requires Microsoft Word installed.

Converting Word to PDF accurately means using Word's own rendering engine, since Word's page layout, fonts, and embedded objects are specific to how Word itself draws them. A browser cannot faithfully replicate that. BenchPDF's desktop app drives your own installed copy of Word directly, so the PDF matches exactly what Word would produce.

How to convert Word to PDF

  • Download and install BenchPDF (free, no account).
  • Open the app and drop your .docx or .doc file, or choose Word to PDF from the grid.
  • BenchPDF opens the document in your installed Word and exports it to PDF.
  • Download the result or open the output folder directly from the app.

Why isn't this available online, only as a desktop download?

Converting Word to PDF accurately means using Word's own rendering engine, since Word's page layout, fonts, and embedded objects are specific to how Word draws them. A browser cannot replicate that. BenchPDF's desktop app drives your own installed copy of Word directly, so the PDF matches exactly, rather than offering a lower-fidelity online approximation.

Is it safe? Does my document get uploaded anywhere?

Yes, it is safe, and no, nothing is uploaded. The desktop app runs entirely on your own PC: it opens your document in your installed copy of Word, exports it to PDF, and saves the result to your disk. There is no server involved in the conversion at any point, and no account is required.

Do I need Microsoft Word installed?

Yes. This conversion drives your own installed copy of Microsoft Word to do the actual rendering, so Word needs to already be on your PC. If it isn't detected, this specific conversion is greyed out in the app with an explanation, rather than failing partway through or silently producing a worse result.

Will it keep my formatting exactly?

Yes. Since the PDF is produced by Word itself rather than a separate reimplementation, fonts, page breaks, headers, footers, and embedded images come out exactly as Word renders them. This avoids the drift that browser-based converters can introduce on complex documents such as multi-column layouts or tracked changes.

Is there a file size limit?

No artificial limit is set by BenchPDF. The practical limit is whatever your own copy of Word can open, since the conversion is Word doing the work. A short letter converts in a couple of seconds. A long document with many images takes Word longer, exactly as it would if you exported it yourself.

Does it work on Mac?

The BenchPDF desktop app is Windows only, since it automates the Windows version of Microsoft Word directly. On a Mac, Word has its own built-in Save as PDF export you can use instead, which produces a similarly faithful result since it is also Word doing the rendering.
