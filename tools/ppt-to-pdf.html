PowerPoint to PDF Converter

This one needs the Windows app. Your own copy of PowerPoint does the export, so the slides look exactly as they do in the deck.

Download BenchPDF 1.0.2 for Windows Needs Microsoft PowerPoint installed.

What comes through

  • Fonts and slide layouts as PowerPoint renders them.
  • Embedded images, charts and shapes, drawn rather than redrawn.
  • Speaker note pages, if you export them from the app.
  • One page per slide, in the deck's order.

Where it stops

A PDF page cannot hold motion, so every animated build is flattened to its finished state, showing the slide as it looks once the animation has played out. Embedded video becomes its poster frame. If a deck relies on things appearing in sequence, the PDF will show all of it at once, which is worth knowing before you send it to someone.

How to convert PowerPoint to PDF

  • Download and install BenchPDF, then open it.
  • Drop your .pptx or .ppt in, or pick PowerPoint to PDF from the grid.
  • BenchPDF opens it in your installed PowerPoint and exports the PDF.
  • Download the result, or open the output folder from the app.

What happens to my animations?

Each slide is flattened to its finished state, so you see everything that would be on screen once the build has finished playing. A PDF page cannot hold motion, so a deck that reveals bullet points one at a time will show them all together in the exported file.

Will my fonts look right?

Yes, because PowerPoint renders the slides using the fonts installed on your PC and embeds the result. That includes brand fonts a browser based converter would substitute. What you see in PowerPoint is what lands in the PDF.

Why is this not in the browser?

Rebuilding a deck's layout, masters, embedded media and theme fonts outside PowerPoint means reimplementing a great deal of PowerPoint. Driving your installed copy sidesteps that entirely and gives you the same output as exporting from PowerPoint yourself.

Do I need PowerPoint installed?

Yes. Your copy does the rendering, so it has to be on the PC. If it is not detected, BenchPDF greys this conversion out and explains why, rather than producing a rougher approximation of your slides without saying so.

Is my presentation uploaded anywhere?

No. The app opens the deck in the PowerPoint already on your PC, exports it, and writes the PDF to your own disk. Nothing is sent to a server during the conversion.

Does it work on a Mac?

The app is Windows only, because it automates the Windows build of PowerPoint. PowerPoint for Mac has its own Save as PDF export which behaves comparably, since PowerPoint is rendering in both cases.
